The Student Advisory Board

A group of student representatives working alongside faculty to improve the undergraduate experience in Art + Design.

The board's mission is to identify student needs, raise concerns, share ideas, and collaborate with faculty to create meaningful impact within the department.

Each semester, SAB hosts two meetings where Board Members, alongside any undergraduate Art + Design student, discuss ways to improve the student experience.

What is a Student Advisor?

Student Advisors are self-nominated advocates for their peers. They represent a wide range of majors, minors, and class years.

During the meetings, they:

  • Listen to feedback from fellow students.
  • Share ideas and concerns with faculty representatives.
  • Collaborate on practical solutions that can improve the department.
  • Help ensure that student voices are included in departmental conversations and decision-making.

Can I serve on SAB?

We encourage students from all undergraduate years to apply, including first-, second-, third-, and fourth-year students. People with diverse experiences, perspectives, and interests are especially encouraged to participate.

We seek representation from students majoring or minoring in:

  • Art History
  • Creative Media
  • Design
  • Games

Everyone is welcome!

SAB meetings are open to all undergraduate Art + Design students. Even if you're not a board member, you're encouraged to attend, share your ideas, and help shape the future of the department. We'd love to hear your perspective! Check your email (or this web page) for updated meeting location and times.
